Maa Chhinnamastike Mandir

Maa Chhinnamastike Mandir, located in Rajrappa, Ramgarh district of Jharkhand, is a renowned Hindu temple dedicated to Goddess Chhinnamasta, one of the ten Mahavidyas in the tantric tradition. The temple is situated at the confluence of the Damodar and Bhairavi rivers and is considered one of the 51 Shakti Peethas, making it a powerful center of Shakti worship. The goddess is uniquely depicted as self-decapitated, holding her severed head in one hand and a scimitar in the other, with three streams of blood flowing from her neck, symbolizing self-sacrifice, spiritual awakening, and the cycle of life and death. This fierce form of the Divine Mother embodies paradoxes such as strength and compassion, death and transcendence. The temple attracts devotees from across India, especially during festivals like Navratri, and is also significant to tribal communities who perform rituals here. The site is known for its tantric rituals, ancient architecture, and the natural beauty of the surrounding waterfalls and rivers, making it both a spiritual and cultural landmark in Jharkhand.

Baba Baidyanath Temple

Baba Baidyanath Temple, located in Deoghar, Jharkhand, is one of the twelve sacred Jyotirlingas dedicated to Lord Shiva and also one of the 51 Shakti Peethas. It is believed to be the place where Sati’s heart fell, making it the Hriday Peeth or Heart Shrine. The temple is famous for its healing powers, with Lord Shiva worshipped here as Baidyanath, the “Lord of Physicians.” Built around the 16th century, the temple attracts millions of devotees, especially during the Shravani Mela when pilgrims carry holy water from the Ganges to offer at the shrine. The temple complex includes the main Jyotirlinga and several other shrines, symbolizing a unique confluence of Shiva and Shakti worship, making it a major spiritual and cultural center in India.



Hundru Water Fall

The Hundru Falls Ranchi is one of the most popular tourist destinations of the city. People coming to the city make sure that they visit the fall located on the Ranchi-Purulia Road during their stay in the city. The Hundru Falls Ranchi is created on the course of the Subarnarekha River, where is falls from a height of 320 feet creating the highest water falls of the state. At the base of the Hundru Waterfall, there is a pool, which serves as a bathing place and a picnic spot. The spectacular scene of water falling from such a great height has been appealing to people for a long time. The different formations of rock due to the erosion by the constantly falling of water have added to the beauty of the place.
